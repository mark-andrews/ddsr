\caption{A non-multilevel model for average alcohol consumption across countries. The $y_1, y_2 \ldots y_n$ variables are alcohol consumption averages for $J$ different countries over different years. As depicted here, we are assuming that $y_1$ and $y_2$ represent averages for the same country, country $j=1$, two different years. The overall average for this country is given by $\mu_1$, whose value is unknown. This non-multilevel model is identical to a one-way anova model with $J$ groups. The common standard deviation term $\sigma$ is simply the homogeneity of variance assumption of the one-way anova.}
